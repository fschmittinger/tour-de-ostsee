FAQ

Good to know

Absolutely not! The bike trip is designed for cycling enthusiasts with different fitness levels. We prioritize fun over speed or endurance. You should have some base fitness as you'll be cycling around 160–200 km in two days — but there will be plenty of breaks, and we'll maintain a comfortable pace to ensure everyone can keep up and have a good time.
Ideally a reliable road bike with clip-in pedals. If you don't have one, you can rent at listnride.com/berlin — highly recommended by colleagues who've used it. If you've never used clip-in pedals, regular cycling shoes are fine too.
No worries. Routes will be planned with train access in mind, so if you ever need to stop early you can catch a train and meet the group at the destination. The goal is for everyone to enjoy the trip at their own pace.
Bring a spare tube and pump — those are the essentials. Beyond that, don't worry: helping each other with mechanicals is just part of the experience, and someone will always be happy to stop.
Hotel and dinner are fully covered. You'll handle your own transport to the Berlin start and back from the Biergarten on Sunday, plus any food and drinks on the road. Further cost details will follow once sign-ups close.
All BCGers are welcome — across Core, Platinion, X, BrightHouse and Quantis. Whether you ride every weekend or are just getting into it, there's a pace for you.
A support car carries your overnight bag between Berlin and the destination, so you only ride with the essentials. Just pack a clearly labelled bag at the start.
Accommodation is organized by the team at the destination, with dinner included. The exact room setup is still being finalised (TBC) and we'll share details once sign-ups close.
Routes run mainly on paved roads and bike paths, with the occasional short gravel stretch. Brandenburg is largely flat, so it's friendly riding — no big climbs to worry about.
Aim to be comfortable riding 60–80 km in a single day before the trip. A few longer rides in the weeks beforehand will make the weekend much more enjoyable — but you don't need to be a racer.
Preparation

What to bring

Pack light, pack smart. Everything you'll need for a smooth two-day ride.

On the Bike
  • Road or gravel bike (in good condition)
  • Helmet — mandatory
  • Cycling gloves
  • Padded shorts / bib shorts
  • Cycling jersey or moisture-wicking top
  • Clip-in shoes or sturdy trainers
  • Sunglasses
Tools & Safety
  • Spare inner tube (at least one)
  • Mini pump or CO₂ inflator
  • Tyre levers & multi-tool
  • Front and rear lights
  • Phone mount & power bank
  • Bike lock
  • Basic first aid kit
Overnight Bag
  • Change of clothes (off-bike)
  • Swimwear — pool dip incoming
  • Toiletries & sunscreen
  • Lightweight rain jacket
  • Warm layer for evenings
  • Sandals or flip-flops
Nutrition
  • Two water bottles
  • Energy bars or gels
  • Snacks for the road
  • Electrolyte tablets
  • Cash for cafés & bakeries en route
Health & Comfort
  • Chamois cream
  • Pain relief / blister plasters
  • Any personal medication
  • Insect repellent
  • Lip balm with SPF
